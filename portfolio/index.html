About Me

Everything that makes me...

  • Wanderlust:

    I love to travel

    I have been traveling since before I can remember. My parents instilled in me a love of exploring and experiencing the world. I have traveled to 43 US States (lived in 7 of them) and 13 countries (lived in 3 of them)! Now that I have my own family, we live for getting out and seeing everything the world has to offer!

  • Photography:

    An eye for scenery

    One of the advantages of growing up and travelling during the Digital Revolution, has been the ability to use so many forms of technology in so many places. I received my first film camera when I was 5 years old and haven't stopped taking pictures since then. My main subjects are landscapes and architecture. All pictures on this site are my own.

  • Event DJ:

    Passion for music and movement

    I have always been a fan of all genres of music and have been collecting albums since CDs were first introduced. Once I had a respectable collection, I began making mixtapes for friends and playing mixes at school events and house parties. Since then I have gone all digital and have been DJing weddings, bars, and other events for more than a decade.

  • Involvement:

    Helping where I can.

    Beginning with mission trips in high school, to remote, impoverished areas of Latin America, helping those less fortunate than myself is in my core. Since then I have done peacekeeping and humanitarian aid missions with the US Army, as well as worked as a mentor in a wide array of settings. One of my drives in the UX field is understanding the needs and helping those in underserved and less fortunate areas.

  • ...and I'm
    still
    going!

Government Website Accessibility

ADA Compliance as part of government website update.

While working for Lochbridge, we contracted with the State of Ohio to redo their Secretary of State website and make it ADA compliant. I was involved in two stages of this project. The first stage was testing all of the individual webpages and assosciated PDF forms to insure that they were fully ADA compliant for Accessibility. The second stage was to begin correcting minor violations on pages through HTML coding and through forms with the PDF layout. This also included fixing and repairing government forms in both English and Spanish.

While I was responsible for both of these phases, my main contribution was working directly with Adobe to understand how to utilize Acrobat Pro to recreate PDF forms in order to be fully accessible.

  • Date: February 2016
  • Client: Ohio Secretary of State
  • Category: Website Accessibility

144th Military Police Company Website

Research military unit heraldry and create website.

While working as the Unit Public Affairs Representative for the 144th Military Police Company, I was assigned to not only create a unit website from scratch, but also to research the lineage and history of the unit. I began by researching the company records, transcribing official documents, scanning photographs dating back to the late 19th Century. After collecting the organization's background information, I created a preliminary mockup of the website and went through several iterations with the company leadership before receiving approval to move forward. I then built the full website once a design was approved. I managed and updated the website from 2012-2014, during my duration in the Public Affairs role.

  • Date: 2012-2014
  • Client: Michigan Army National Guard
  • Category: Web Design
